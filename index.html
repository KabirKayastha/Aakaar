Aakaar

In its nascent form, an artwork is simply an image or an observation, existing as musings in our thoughts or as draft sketches within our journals. Yet, when fully realized, there remains a need to communicate with more witnesses outside our immediate circle.

AAKAAR, an exhibition assembled by the second year students of Kathmandu University School of Arts, Department of Art and Design, stems from the aforementioned idea along with a discerned necessity for incorporating an exhibition as a crucial part of the students' curriculum.

This exhibit is a compilation of our individual working processes in terms of techniques and materials in both indoor and outdoor settings from the first semester of our second year. They are an inquisitive call for feedback and interaction with our academic circle as well as a broader audience.

We focus on a range of portraiture, still life, landscape and cityscapes in our academic exploration through the use of composition, draftsmanship and colors. Moreover, our relatively liberal approaches to photography, woodcut prints, pottery and sculptures embody our creative efforts throughout the semester.

Through our exhibition, we wish to welcome more perspectives into our efforts and reflect on new meanings they may generate.
